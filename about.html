The Foundation

Built for Riders,
by Riders

Riders Ranch Bikers Club is based in the heart of the Kingdom of Eswatini at Sidvokodvo — a town in the Manzini Region sitting in the scenic Middleveld terrain.

Founded in 1966, the club was built to give older riders a reason to get out, stay active, and share the road. Cycling has no age limit — and neither does adventure.

That bike on the stone wall at our entrance gate says everything about what this place stands for. It's been our landmark since the beginning, and it always will be.

The Landscape

Riding in Eswatini

🏝

The Middleveld

Sidvokodvo sits in Eswatini's Middleveld — 500m to 1000m elevation, rolling hills, and some of the finest cycling roads in Southern Africa.

🌞

The Climate

Subtropical warmth, mild dry winters. Sunday morning rides benefit from cool, clear air before the day heats up — near-perfect conditions most of the year.

🛣

The Roads

Quiet rural routes, light traffic, breathtaking scenery. Eswatini is one of Southern Africa's best-kept cycling secrets and Riders Ranch has every route mapped.

🌎

The Neighbours

Bordered by South Africa and Mozambique — both feature on the annual tour calendar. New roads, new challenges, same tight-knit crew.

Our Journey

A History of Riding

1966

The Club is Born

Cycling enthusiasts in Sidvokodvo form Riders Ranch with a shared mission: to keep riding, stay adventurous, and share the roads of what was then Swaziland.

1970s

First Organised Events

Sunday rides formalised. First structured time trials begin. Members start competing across the region, putting Riders Ranch on the local cycling map.

1980s

The Swazi Rally Era

The club participates in the Swazi Rally and helps grow it into an iconic event. First overnight tours begin. The adventurous identity of Riders Ranch is cemented.

1990s

Three Groups Established

Fast, Medium, and Easy groups formalised. Every rider, at every level, now has a home. The Sunday lunch tradition becomes sacred.

2000s

Annual Dinner Tradition

The year-end awards dinner becomes a club institution. Record holders honoured, Rider of the Year crowned, season celebrated in style.

2018

The Eswatini Era

The Kingdom of Swaziland is officially renamed the Kingdom of Eswatini. Riders Ranch proudly rides on under the new national identity.

Today

Still Rolling Strong

58+ years in, the club keeps growing. New members, new records, new tour destinations — same gate, same roads, same spirit.
